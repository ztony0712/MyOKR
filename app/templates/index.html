What is OKR

OKR (Objectives and Key Results) is a set of management tools and methods to define and track the completion of targets. It is also a simple and effective system for companies to manage their objectives from the top down to the grassroots level.

How to implement OKR

(1) Goal setting. (annual targets, quarterly targets, starting with strategy) (2) Define the KRs for each goal (from quarterly goals to "key results" breakdown) (3) Drive implementation (from key results to "action plans") (4) Regular review.

Basic requirements for formulation

(1) A maximum of five O's with a maximum of four KRs per O. (2) All must be collaborative, no command forms can appear. (3) A score of 0.6-0.7 is a good performance, so 0.6-0.7 will be your target. (4) Someone to ensure that everyone is marching towards the same goals.

OKR Principles

(1) OKRs should be quantifiable (time & quantity). (2) Goals are ambitious and challenging. (3) Each person's OKRs are transparent to the whole company. Each person's introduction page should contain a record of their OKRs, including content and ratings.

Frequently Asked Questions


Why use OKR?

It can focus on a sense of purpose across the company. OKRs provide a common basis of accountability for everyone, no matter what their focus is. Ability to shortcomings focus and prioritise; OKRs provide a concrete way to give everyone an understanding of what is actually being focused and prioritised.

What is the main difference between OKR and KPI?

OKR is not an appraisal tool, but a more directive one, as opposed to KPI. The main purpose of OKR is not to assess a team or an employee, but to remind everyone what the task at hand is.

What can OKR do for me?

OKRs develop the discipline to think and plan for the long term. For managers, putting goals on the page clarifies expectations. For others, OKRs provide transparency in terms of topics and priorities and facilitate collaboration between tasks.

What are the disadvantages of OKR?

(1) Requires a high level of overall personnel quality and involvement. (2) The objectives are focused on short-term settings. (3) Requires a certain amount of time and energy and resources from each person.
